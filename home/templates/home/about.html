The point of Math Orientation is to help you make the transition to university life! Over the week, you will learn more about what courses are offered at UW, become familiar with the campus, make new friends, and learn what it means to be a member of the Faculty of Mathematics. Whatever your story, Math Orientation will provide a lot of events to help you learn more about your academics, your environment and your fellow students!

Orientation Hierarchy

Front-line leaders

The Pink and Black ties are the hard-working soldiers at the head of the Kingdom's mighty army. Each of these leaders are trained in arts of swordsmanship, engagement, and pie evasion. If there's anything you need done, these mighty warriors are your go-to gals and guys.

Pink Ties

Pink-Tie leaders are the ones interacting with you on the front-lines, building the orientation experience, and particpating alongside you. They will be helping your team gain fame and glory on the fields of justice as you compete for the Math Orientation week trophy.

Black Ties

Black-Tie leaders work behind the scenes to make the Math Orientation week magic happen. They are responsible for setting up, running, and tearing down events.

MODs

High above on MOD Olympus, the MODs and their magical logistical powers protect the Kingdom from the forces of evil, disorder, and poor taste. They are wicked-smart and show it by coming up every single battle plan. They provide everything you could possibly need to make orientation safe, memorable, and enjoyable by everyone. Look out for our symbol and be sure to say hi when you see them. They may have a tasty treat for you.

Teamsters

Teamsters are rarely seen, but without them, Math Orientation would not be possible. They ensure the transportation of supplies, electronics, food, etc. to various faculty events. If you do catch a glimpse of them, make sure to say hi (they also have candy!).

Media

Media members are easily recognizable by their faces glued to a camera. They work behind the scenes taking pictures and videos throughout Math Orientation, documenting the entire week in all its glory. If you think you've been snapped, hop by our Facebook page as we'll try to post our pictures and videos throughout the week.

Devisors

Devisors make the events possible. They spend their pre-week time planning and coordinating the events that run during Math Orientation week, then take charge and coordinate leaders during the day to make sure things run smoothly.

Tie Guard

Tie Guards guard the Pink-Tie outside M3 during the week to ensure it is protected 24/7. They are a central hub of information for you throughout the week; if you're lost or just want to say hi, stop by, they're very friendly.

Math FOC

Your Math Federation Orientation Committee (FOC) that coordinate everyone mentioned above!
